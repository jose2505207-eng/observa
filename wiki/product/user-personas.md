# User Personas

Who OBSERVA is built for. These guide feature priorities and [[accessibility-principles]].

## Primary: Totally blind, independent traveler
- Uses a screen reader (TalkBack) fluently; high expectations for non-visual UX.
- Needs hazard/obstacle awareness and orientation help in unfamiliar spaces.
- Values privacy and works in places with no/unreliable connectivity.
- Cannot rely on "aim the camera" interactions. Drives [[ambient-awareness]] and [[spatial-guidance]].

## Primary: Low-vision user
- Has partial sight; uses magnification and high contrast.
- Wants text read aloud ([[ocr-mode]]) and confirmation of what's ahead.
- Benefits from both audio and (large/high-contrast) visual cues.

## Secondary: Newly blind / lower tech confidence
- Less comfortable with complex gestures.
- Needs a simple, forgiving voice-first flow. Drives [[voice-command-layer]].

## Secondary: Multilingual user
- Encounters signage/text in a language they don't read.
- Drives on-device **translation** as part of [[ocr-mode]].

## Current Reality
No persona-specific features are implemented yet. Personas inform the roadmap ([[hackathon-mvp]]).
